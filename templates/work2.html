002 : Main Statistical Functions in Data Science Project

There are many data students who constantly ask if it is necessary to learn statistics to be a good data scientist. The answer is YES, we have to know statistics to be a differentiated professional. But the good news is that we don’t have to become a statistics master. We simply need the essentials to be able to evaluate a database. We will see some statistical analysis of data that a scientist needs to do before starting to select and clean the research dataset. We will analyze a dataset of customer time and expenses in the app of an Ecommerce company.

1) Analyzing the pandas.describe() function

The describe function helps the data scientist to understand a little bit about the dataset. It contains formulas such as information count (lines), mean, standard deviation, minimum value, percentage of data (25%, 50%, 75%) and maximum value. We will analyze the column “amount spent annually” from the dataset below:










First, we can verify that we have 500 rows or records in our dataset. This information is provided by the count shown in the first row of the describe table. The count is very important for statistical calculations, especially since many of them are divided by the general sample of information.

In the second line we have the mean (arithmetic mean) which is the sum of all values ​​in the column, divided by the number of a samples which would be the count. So we can see that the average annual spending on the app is US$ 499.31.

In the third line we have the std (Standard Deviation) which is a measure that expresses the degree of dispersion of a data set. It would be how much the data deviates from the mean. To calculate the standard deviation, it is necessary to add all the data from position 1 to position n, subtract each value from the mean of the set and square it, then divide by the total amount of data from the set (count). On the fourth line of our describe function we have min which is the minimum amount spent by all customers. So we can analyze that no one spent less than US$ 256.67 in a period of 1 year. This low number may be due to the customer being a new member of our app. As we can see in the Member Years column, the customer became a member less than three months ago.

In the fifth, sixth and seventh line, we can analyze that up to 25% of customers spend an amount of US$ 445.03 per year. We can consider that a quarter of customers spend almost the average annual amount. With these percentages, we observe that the longer the member, the more customers spend.

On the last line, we can see that the highest amount spent annually was US$ 765.51, by an old customer who has been a member for over 6 years.

1) Analyzing the pandas.corr() function

The correlation function defines very well which columns a variable is identified with. We can see below that the Amount spent annually column is positively correlated with the Years of membership column. This means that the longer the customer is a member of the app, the more likely they are to spend on Ecommerce. We can also analyze that customers buy more in the store using the app instead of the website.

Conclusion

The data scientist’s biggest challenge is understanding the business problem. You don’t have to be a math major or take multiple statistics courses. It is enough simply to understand where the pieces fit together. The describe function is one of the most important functions in the data world. In it, we can learn more about company and customer information, and may even help to identify outliers that are aberrant value or atypical value (age==200 or height==-1.50).
